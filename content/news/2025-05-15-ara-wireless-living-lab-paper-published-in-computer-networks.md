<p>The paper <strong><a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.comnet.2025.111188" target="_blank" rel="noopener">&ldquo;Design and Implementation of ARA Wireless Living Lab for Rural Broadband and Applications&rdquo;</a></strong> (Taimoor Ul Islam et al.) has been published in <em>Computer Networks</em>, Vol. 263, Article 111188 (May 2025). <a href="https://arawireless.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ARA</a> is a first-of-its-kind wireless living lab for rural broadband research, deployed across farms, communities, and roads in central Iowa, integrating high-capacity x-haul, programmable base stations, and even low-earth-orbit satellite links to support applications from precision agriculture to rural connectivity. A free, publicly available version is posted as an <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.00913" target="_blank" rel="noopener">arXiv preprint</a>.</p>

<p>ARA is a great example of our infrastructure work reaching beyond Chameleon: the testbed uses a derivative of <a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/experiment/chiedge/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">CHI@Edge</a> to manage its experiments, extending the same edge-to-cloud experiment model we described in our <a href="https://doi.org/10.1145/3708035.3736014" target="_blank" rel="noopener">PEARC&rsquo;25 CHI@Edge paper</a> to a live, at-scale rural wireless environment. If you are interested in running experiments on real edge hardware, you can learn more about CHI@Edge and get started on the <a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/experiment/chiedge/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Chameleon site</a>.</p>
