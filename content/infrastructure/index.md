<h1>Nimbus Infrastructure</h1>
<p>Nimbus infrastructure served as one of the first open source implementations of the concept of Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS) with the production release of the first component, the <a href="https://wordpress.cels.anl.gov/nimbusproject/wp-content/uploads/sites/116/2023/08/VW_EuroPar05.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Workspace Service</a>, released in mid-2005. Over time, the system grew to add an implementation of a <a href="https://wordpress.cels.anl.gov/nimbusproject/wp-content/uploads/sites/116/2023/08/bresnahan_sciencecloud2011.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">scalable quota-based storage cloud</a>, <a href="https://wordpress.cels.anl.gov/nimbusproject/wp-content/uploads/sites/116/2023/08/contextualization-eScience2008.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">contextualization tools</a>, allowing users to configure &ldquo;one click&rdquo; virtual clusters, as well as a variety of <a href="https://wordpress.cels.anl.gov/nimbusproject/wp-content/uploads/sites/116/2023/08/keahey_wcs_ocs_2012.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">tools creating and managing configurations distributed over multiple clouds</a>, and adapting them to the <a href="https://wordpress.cels.anl.gov/nimbusproject/wp-content/uploads/sites/116/2023/08/Science-Clouds-CCA08.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">needs of the scientific community</a>. Nimbus software was used to configure multiple research clouds (FutureGrid being the most prominent example) as well as enable a variety of scientific applications.</p>

<p>While pioneering and committed to a quality implementation, Nimbus remained primarily a research project. In the early 2010s, the <a href="https://openstack.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">OpenStack IaaS</a> cloud implementation emerged as a viable alternative to Nimbus, with strong support from the open source community. To better leverage the momentum behind cloud-related development, the Nimbus team transitioned to become an OpenStack contributor, always advocating the needs of the scientific community, and significantly contributing to OpenStack services such as <a href="https://docs.openstack.org/blazar/latest/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Blazar</a> reflecting our community&rsquo;s requirements.</p>

<p>Today, the Nimbus team leads the operation of the <a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Chameleon research cloud</a>, an OpenStack-based testbed for computer science systems research. While the Nimbus project is no longer under active development, the Nimbus team continues to drive science-related features into cloud computing development via contributions in research, development, and operations. Aside from operating the Chameleon testbed, the Nimbus team <a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/about/papers-and-tech-reports/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">actively contributes</a> to exploring topics such as auto-scaling, preemptible workloads, and the use of clouds to advance reproducibility in science.</p>
